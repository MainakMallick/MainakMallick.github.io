I'm a 1st Year Graduate Student of Computational Science and Engineering Department at Georgia Tech. I am currently working on Time series Forecasting on MultiModal Dataset using Transformer and Fault Classification Using Meta Learning For Highly Imbalanced Dataset under the guidance of Prof. Seung-Kyum Choi.

My research interests include multi-modal data forecasting, meta-learning, Imitation Learning, foundation models, computer vision, natural language processing (NLP), few-shot learning, digital twins, and predictive maintenance. I am also keen on 3D computer vision, volumetric occupancy prediction, with a growing focus on robotics/ Embodied AI—particularly in robot arm control, visual decision-making, task and motion planning (TAMP), and long-horizon decision making in complex, real-world environments.

Previously, I worked as a Data Scientist at HSBC Bank, where I maintained and optimized decision systems for the Collections and Recoveries Portfolio. My role involved developing data-driven models to enhance risk assessment, automate workflows, and improve financial decision-making.
